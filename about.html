About Aaron Riddle

Background

Forged in Darkness,
Built to Endure

Aaron Riddle doesn't do small. His work lives on full backs, wraps around throats, and consumes entire legs — commanding attention from every angle. A specialist in Neo-Japanese, Dark Ornamental, and Black & Gray tattooing, Aaron has built a reputation for pieces that feel less like ink and more like ancient artifacts unearthed from shadow.

"Every piece I create is a collaboration between darkness and intention. The skin is the canvas, but the story belongs to the person wearing it."

Drawing from Japanese mythology, sacred geometry, and the raw theatricality of biomechanical design, Aaron's portfolio reads like the pages of a forbidden manuscript — demon faces locked in perpetual fury, skulls with ember-lit eyes, ornamental compositions that transform the body into living architecture.

His color work is restrained and deliberate — fire and ember accents that glow against vast fields of black and gray, giving each piece an inner light that photographs can barely capture. His white-ink-over-blackwork technique has become a signature, creating depth and luminescence that defies expectation.

Beyond the tattoo chair, Aaron is a dedicated educator — conducting seminars and workshops for artists looking to push their own boundaries in dark, large-scale tattooing. His teaching philosophy mirrors his art: precision first, ambition always.

Currently booking select clients for custom, large-scale projects. No flash. No compromise. Only work that stands the test of time.
